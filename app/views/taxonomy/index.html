The language we use when communicating within DWP

The terms and language we use within DWP Health to communicate between ourselves and with our internal DWP stakeholders.

  • Life event
    A specific event in a person's life - can be long-term of short-term - these are the trigger points for someone looking for a service.

  • Service
    A service helps someone do something (achieve a goal). A whole end-to-end serice delivers outcomes for its users.

  • Benefits and grants
    Financial support awarded and discharged by DWP to certain people on low incomes or to meet specific needs.

  • Actors
    People using or delivering the service.

  • User journey
    Describes the user's experience throughout the end-to-end service, and beyond.

  • Channels
    How actors interact with the service.

  • Service phases
    We can break services into key phases of the overall process.

  • Business capabilities
    The ability or capacity required by the business to fulfill the activities needed to support our service.

  • Teams
    How we organise our resources and capacity.

  • Application product
    A group of technical components that coming to support the delivery of a service or services.

  • Technology
    Digital platforms, products, hardware, application software - things we build, maintain and buy.

  • Data
    The actual information that's either generated by or used to carry our activities and services.
