Current artefacts

Our goals

Our goals reflect our passions, our principles and our dedication to user-centred design. They’re what we’ll focus on to make our work, ourselves and our community better.


Measures of success

Measures of success are how we validate we’re doing the right thing when we create or iterate design solutions.


Definition of done

A definition of done checklist of things that should be completed before a piece of work is considered ‘done’.


Backlog

Priority 1

The first things we will create are:

  • measures of success (how we validate that we're doing the right things)
  • goals (clearly defined, and aligned)
  • a Definition of Done (a list of things you should have done before you 'finish' a piece of content design to ensure consistent quality standards)
  • content architecture (how we start taking a content-first approach, instead of retrospectively adding content in empty boxes)
  • logging design decisions (best practice, examples, suggested templates)

Priority 2 - to do next

The next things we will create are:

  • 2i best practice (why, when and how to do 2i)
  • content debt (what it is, how we manage it, how it ties into research priorities)
  • guidance on how we use the tools available for content design (style guides, design systems, and strategy for improving them)
  • Health Terminology A-Z (language and terms unique to DWP Health)
  • a statement of purpose (what is a content strategy, how does it support the work we're doing in health, how content fits into the bigger picture)

Priority 3 - to review later

    Later, we will review and reprioritise:

  • principles for how we work (ways of working, promoting positive culture)
  • Content Crit guidance (why, when and how to do a content crit)
  • section to show our practice maturity (what we've achieved so far, where we are now, what we still have to do)
  • milestones to review and update the strategy (should be an evolving artefact, not once and done)
  • guidance on how we work with other areas (for example working with SPD, or external comms)
